This document simply details a ‘blind’ visual and basic numeric assessment of the images.

NOTE: I am accustomed to black features on a white background so the images may be inverted in comparison to what you are used to.

FC3344_LOBE_LANEA_0--Stage20--488.tif (the second image is explored in the Appendix)

Stored in a 16 bit tif but only around 10% (7000 levels) of the dynamic range used in the bulk of the image. Green background above is everything at 65000 and above and red is everything at 58000 and below.

In this image the sub arrays have a raised background that allows relatively easy segregation from the image background.

A basic segmentation into features, subarray background and global background can be achieved with thresholds of 63500 and 65000

There is evidence of rotation of the arrays within the image.

This appears to be a ‘whole image alignment whilst imaging’ effect and not an array manufacture issue.

There are smaller subarrays between the larger arrays which I assume are reference areas but they look inconsistent within the image and the grids are not distinct.

Also evidence of defocus/blurring towards the edges probably due to the camera optics. It would be useful to know the depth of field as this could be a result of the chip not being exactly perpendicular.

The array features are Gaussian-like spikes around 6 pixels in diameter.

Generally the features remain on the grid pattern but there is some evidence of off grid alignment and bleed into neighbours

There is a broad range of intensities which visually makes it unclear what is a ‘positive’ and what is a ‘negative’

Once we have extracted the features we can look to see if there is a dual peaked distribution but we may want to consider obtaining more optimal images so we can explore the ‘ground truth’ more clearly as the imaging properties may be masking the results. The global and subarray grey level histograms show no distinct sub distributions but this is to be expected from a group of Gaussian features.

Summary

Although it may add expense to the unit I believe a major improvement in the data may be obtained by looking at the hardware aspects of the imaging. This may also impact the speed of the instrument (but this depends on if the array moves through the system or stays in place during the probe cycles as simply enabling more than one sample/array at a time could use the imaging down time due to other processes for an overall higher efficiency).

The first goal must be in increasing the grey level range. This may involve exploring:

  • Alternative fluorophores
  • Alternative illumination and it’s intensity
  • The camera used and its associated optics
  • Exposure times (overall scan time penalty)
  • Software driven adaptive capture with multiple settings (overall scan time penalty)

The second goal will be to tackle the rotation of the image and the blurring towards the edges. This may involve exploring:

  • Taking a global image and then moving the array to more optimally image each subarray (this could greatly increase capture times)
  • Rotation adjustment possible in optics but software options will be explored here
  • Multiple camera systems or alternative technologies such as line scan cameras
  • Checking the depth of field of the camera and that the chip is perfectly perpendicular

Analysis goals (separate document)

Initially the focus will be on locating the feature areas within the images reliably.

  • Explore masks to clean up the data (a tuned variant of a DoG will be fast and should clean things up a bit)
  • Find the features in the subarrays (initially focus on rotation and alignment of the features and not the image data itself)
  • Extract the pad results (probably just pixels within an area)
  • Look at different summary measures for the extracted pad pixels
  • Look for ‘positive’ versus ‘negative’ distributions and hence thresholds
  • Identify pads where the Gaussian is not centred or appears to overflow in to neighbours (flag potentially bad data)
  • For now ignore the edge blur and grey level limitations
