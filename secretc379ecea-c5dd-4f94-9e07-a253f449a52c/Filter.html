Filter Explorations

Simple explorations for filters. This one simply chains Gaussian filters together and then takes the blurred image as ‘noise and background’. It could do with tuning and works better in some cases than others. Sometimes it can be hard to tell without looking in detail at the image.

This filter is probably too severe and is better as a filter in front of alignment to improve the probability of that working and once the images are aligned more powerful cross image techniques could be used.

It is interesting but not earth shattering. More tuning on more images would be required.

I may look at a DoG filter on the halos more and a few alternative approaches.

Look in the About section for libraries and default parameters.

  image_gray = cv.imread(imageDir+imageFileStub,cv.IMREAD_ANYDEPTH )
  img_gray_255=np.uint8(PercentileScaledImage(image_gray,lowerPerc=0.1,upperPerc=99.9,maxVal=255,invert=False))
  
  filtered_image = gaussian(image_gray, 1,preserve_range=True)
  filtered_image = gaussian(filtered_image,2,preserve_range=True)
  filtered_image = gaussian(filtered_image,3,preserve_range=True)
  filtered_image = gaussian(filtered_image,4,preserve_range=True)

  filtered_image = gaussian(filtered_image,8,preserve_range=True)
  
  img_gray_255_1=np.uint8(PercentileScaledImage(filtered_image,lowerPerc=0.1,upperPerc=99.9,maxVal=255,invert=False))
  
  diff_image=image_gray-filtered_image
  
  img_gray_255_2=np.uint8(PercentileScaledImageToImage(diff_image,filtered_image,lowerPerc=0.1,upperPerc=99.9,maxVal=255,invert=False))

  img1=255-img_gray_255
